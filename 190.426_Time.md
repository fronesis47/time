### Catalog Description

This undergraduate seminar explores the philosophical concept of time as it relates to contemporary theories of politics. We will read mainly a selection of works form the philosophical canon (Plato, Descartes, Kant, Heidegger, Derrida, Ricoeur) along with works from contemporary theorists (Brown, Connolly, Grosz, Honig, Rancière).


### Assessment<!-- from money sem -->

*Active Participation •* 30%

Attendance is necessary but not sufficient; you must engage frequently, actively, and edifyingly in both seminars and tutorials.

*“Mark-ups” (x4)* • 20%

You must submit 4 “mark-ups” (1 before first exam date) of the readings, with marginal notes, highlights and highlight explanations. Due *prior to* start of the class that covers the readings. 

*Tutorial Paper* • 15%

10-minutes in length, read aloud; written version due at 10pm the night before the tutorial.

*Exam 1* • 15%

10-minute oral examination

*Exam 2* • 20%

15-minute oral examination

Nothing in the course can be “made up” after the fact and no out-of-class “work” can substitute for in-class performance. Participation requires presence, by definition. The tutorial paper and exams will be performed in class. Only in the case of an extreme, *documented* medical emergency or death in the family, will alternative arrangements be considered.
