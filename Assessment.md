## Assessment

*Active Participation •* 20%

- Attendance is necessary but not sufficient; you must engage frequently, actively, and edifyingly, both in small-group work and the wider seminar.

*“Mark-ups” (x5)* • 15%

- You must submit 5 “mark-ups” of the readings, with marginal notes, highlights and highlight explanations. Due *prior to* start of the class that covers the readings. Of your 5 total, 2 are due before Exam 1; 2 are due between Exam 1 and Exam 2; 1 is due after Exam 2. For more details, see [markups](MarkUp.md).

*In-Class Exegeses (x5)* • 15%

- On 5 different occasions we will begin class with an exegesis exercise: you will be given a short portion of the text we read (anywhere from a sentence to a paragraph) and asked to write a detailed exegesis (anywhere from 100 to 500 words) that explains and elaborates the text. 

*Oral Exam 1* • 15%

- 10-minute oral examination

*Oral Exam 2* • 15%

- 10-minute oral examination

*Written Final Exam* • 20%

- In-class, closed notes, synthetic essays on all material covered in seminar. 


### Important Note on Course Requirements 
Nothing in the course can be “made up” after the fact and no out-of-class “work” can substitute for in-class performance. Participation requires presence, by definition. The exegeses paper and exams will all be performed in class. Only in the case of an extreme, *documented* medical emergency or death in the family, will alternative arrangements be considered.
